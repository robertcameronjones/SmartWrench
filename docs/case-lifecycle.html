Service Booking — How It Works

A plain‑English walkthrough of how Kate gets a customer from a detected service need to a confirmed appointment, and what happens after.

Kate is the friendly scheduling assistant for our dealer partners. When a customer's vehicle needs service, Kate reaches out, helps them pick a time, reminds them the day before, and follows up after the appointment. She works over voice or SMS, whichever the customer prefers.

Where the work begins — Triggers

A new case opens when something tells us a vehicle needs attention. The three trigger sources:

Recall

A manufacturer recall applies to one of the dealer's vehicles.

DTC

The vehicle's onboard diagnostic system flags a fault code that needs attention.

Regular maintenance due

Scheduled service is coming up based on time, mileage, or manufacturer interval.

Triggers go into a queue. We work one case per customer at a time — if a customer already has an open case, any new trigger for them waits its turn.

The main path — what a happy case looks like

STEP 1
Contact
Kate reaches out and the customer responds.
STEP 2
Book
Customer agrees and picks an open slot.
STEP 3
Confirm
Kate repeats the slot back; customer confirms; dealer confirms availability.
STEP 4
Initial reminder
24 hours before the appointment, Kate texts a reminder. Customer can confirm, reschedule, cancel, or stay silent.
STEP 5
Final reminder
Day of (a couple of hours before the slot), Kate texts again with the same four options — even if the initial reminder went unanswered.
STEP 6
Arrive
Guidepoint's GPS confirms arrival at the dealership. Customer can still text in to reschedule right up to the appointment.
STEP 7
Feedback
After the appointment, Kate texts to ask how it went.
→→→→→→✓

Each reminder has the same four customer outcomes — confirm, reschedule, cancel, no response. Confirming or staying silent at the initial reminder doesn't advance or close the case — Kate acknowledges warmly and the final reminder still fires on the day of the appointment. Only cancel or reschedule at either reminder ends or restarts the case immediately. The final outcome (showed / no-show / completed) is decided by the geofence and end-of-business-day, not by the customer's replies to the reminders.

How a case can end

YesBooked & serviced

Customer agreed, came in, got serviced. We close the case after a feedback note.

NoCustomer declined

The customer says no — at any step. We thank them and close the case. We don't reach out again about this same issue.

MaybeNo firm answer

The customer engaged but didn't commit. We give them 24 hours, then try once more. If still no firm answer, we close the case.

SilentNo response at all

For SMS, we wait 24 hours, send one more message, then close after another 24 hours of silence (48 hours total).

RescheduleCustomer wants a different time

At either reminder, or any time before the appointment, the customer can ask to move it. We pick a new slot under the same case and reset both reminders for the new date.

CancelledCustomer cancels the appointment

If the customer cancels outright at either reminder (not a reschedule), we treat it as a decline and close the case.

No-showVehicle didn't arrive

Our GPS sees no arrival at the dealership during the appointment window. Case closes. (No automatic reach‑back, today.)

How Kate uses voice and SMS

The customer chooses the channel

Each customer has a preferred way to be reached — voice or SMS. Kate uses that channel for the booking conversation.

Voice gets one phone call

If Kate calls and the conversation doesn't land in a clear yes or no, the rest of the case continues over SMS. We never call a second time.

Reminders, arrival check, and feedback are always SMS — they're short, async, and respectful of people's time.
